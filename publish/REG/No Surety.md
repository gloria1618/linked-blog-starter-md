creditor lends $ to debtor, debtor defaults, no surety/collateral
creditors w/o security interest/mortgage in debtor's prop can gain rights in debtor's prop thru imposition of lien or garnishment

Prejudgment Attachment
- before final judgement in suit on debt is rendered, if creditor has reason to believe that debtor will not pay (and lien dissapearing after winning lawsuit??)
	- creditor can ask court to provisionally attach piece of debtors property
		- court then issues writ of attachment (to local sheriff), and
		- prop is seized so a creditor who prevails will be assured of recovering on judgement thru sale of prop

Legal Options (Post Judgement)
- judicial Lien
	- put lien on prop debtor is in possession of
	- if debtor adjudged to owe creditor $ and judgement has gone unsatisfied:
		- creditor can request court impose lien on specific prop owned and possessed by debtor
	- after court imposes lien, it will issue a writ (usually to local sheriff) to
		- seize prop belonging to debtor
		- sell it
		- turn over proceeds to creditor
	- homestead exemption - why ppl owing $ move to FL
		- most states protect certain prop to prevent destitute debtor
		- excludes items of persons household, up to certain amt, from liens of most creditors
		- applies
			- *general* creditor lends $ to debtor who refuses to pay
			- creditor cannot seize house
		- does not apply
			- to persons w purchase money security interests (PMSIs) in personal prop or purchase money mortgages against real prop
				- bank lent $, but if u dont pay we're seizing house
			- when txpyr fails to pay federal taxes, IRS can file lien on all of txpyrs prop incl prop exempt from levy under state law
	- Mechanic & Artisans Liens
		- under [[CL]] mechanic/artisan working on prop and either improves/repairs it automatically has lien on prop for price of repairs - for as long as prop is in lienor's (mechanic's) possession
		- these liens are possessory
			- dissolve as soon as lienor lets owner have prop back
			- thats why mechanic wont give car back
		- if mechanic/artisan/innkeeper/etc goes unpaid, they may give owner notice of intention to sell retained prop to pay owner's bill
		- materialman's lien
			- imposed in favor of contractors who perform work on, or provide supplies for, real prop improvements
			- unpaid materialman must file notice w local recorder of deeds in order to preserve their lien for when house is sold
- Garnishment
	- garnish debtor's prop in hands of 3rd party
	- debtor is adjudged to owe creditor $ and debtor has prop in hands of a 3rd party, a writ of garnishment may be sought
	- writ orders person holding prop to:
		- turn it over to creditor, or
		- be held personally L for value of prop not turned over
	- fed law provides that SS pmts are not subj to:
		- garnishment, execution, levy, or attachment
		- states often limit amt of emplees wages that may be garnished to prevent destitute debtor 

Fraudulent Conveyances
- important concept in bankruptcy law
- when debtor transfers prop w intent to hide, delay, defraud creditors
- fraudulent conveyances void/voidable and will be set aside in proper proceeding (probs go to jail too)
- in det if fraudulent conveyance occured, court will consider whether
	- transfer was to insider (eg relative, business partner, officer, director)
	- debtor retained possession/control of prop transferred
	- transfer was not disclosed/concealed
	- transfer was of substantially all the debtors assets
	- value received by debtor for A was unreasonable
	- debtor was insolvent/became insolvent shortly after transfer

Fair Debt Collection Practices Act (FDCPA)
- curbs abuses by collection agencies in collecting consumer debts
- doesnt apply to creditor attempting to collect own debts; j to services that collect consumer debts for others
- Prohibited Acts
	- act severely restricts collection agencies' ability to call 3rd parties, such as relatives of debtor, to indirectly pressure
	- collection agency can contract 3rd persons to discover debtor's whereabouts, but may not disclose:
		- that it is a collection agency, or
		- debtor owes a debt
	- contacting debtor at inconvenient/unusual times; in most cases 'convenient' times are bw 8am n 9pm
	- contacting debtor directly if debtor repped by attorney
	- using harassing/abusive language to debtor
	- making false/misleading claims
	- contracting debtor at their place of emplmt if emplyr objects
- Remedies Under Act: Debtor's Power to Terminate Contracts
	- debtor has power to terminate collection agency's contracts by notifying agency in writing
		- that debtor will not pay debt, and
		- to stop further comms
	- agency must stop comms except to inform debtor that it is:
		- bringing lawsuit
		- seeking other remedies
- Remedies Under the Act: Damages
	- FDCPA gives debtors right to sue for actual damages caused by collection agency's misconduct
	- FDCPA provides statutory 1k damage award
