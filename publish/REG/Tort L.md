Tort - wrongful act
- Negligence (unintentional)
	- L for compensatory damages
- Fraud (intentional)
	- L for compensatory n punitive damages

Respondeat Superior
- as gen rule, principal not L for torts committed by agent - only agent L
- Exception for emplyrs:
	- under respondeat superior doctrine, emplyr can be L for emplee's torts committed w/in [[scope of emplmt]]
		- doesn't relieve agent of L
		- injured person may sue both emplyr under repondeat superior n agent
- Emplyr is
	- L only for torts of emplee (or servant)
	- usually not L for torts of indep contractors unless
		- emplyr authorized act
		- work involved ultra hazardous activity

Emplyr - Emplee Relationship
- most important factor in det whether person is emplee or independent contractor is:
	- right of principal to control manner in which person performs
	- indep contractors - emplyr has little control over methods used
	- emplee - emplyr has right to control methods used and manner of work
- additional factors: where right to control unclear, courts look at
	- whether worker has business of own (c)
	- provides their own tools/facilities (c)
	- length of emplmt (short/definite (c) vs long/indefinite (e))
	- basis of compensation
		- time (e), given amt for job (c)
	- degree of supervision
