Liquidation: Trustee Appointed

Trustee does following:
- collects debtor's assets
- liquidates them
- uses proceeds to pay off creditors to extent possible

- if debtor indiv/married couple, debtors debts discharged, w certain exceptions
	- Exceptions: WAFTED (see below)
- if debtor is entity, it is dissolved
	- no discharge given, but effect is same - debts wiped

Dismissal/Conversion of Ch7 Case
- Ch 7 case by indiv consumer debtor may be dismissed (or w debtor's consent converted to [[Ch 13]] case)
	- upon finding that granting relief under Ch 7 would constitute abuse, or
	- bc debtor has sufficient inc to pay debts 

Step 1: Compare Debtor's Monthly Inc to State Median Inc
- if indiv filing for Ch7 liquidation and their spouse have monthly inc > state median income for fam of same size, then state/interested creditor/court may file motion to dismiss case, either:
	- under means test
	- for gen abuse bc debtor has sufficient inc to pay debts

Step 2: Means Test
- to det whether creditor would be better off under Ch13, 5yr reorg
- debtors 5yr projected inc = (avg monthly inc - allowed exp) * 60
- debtors 5yr projected inc compared w high/low threshold (10,275;17,150)
	- if debtors inc is inbw, more calc involved, beyond scope of exam

Objections to Discharge
- creditors often want to prevent Ch7 discharge
- Code prov 2 kinds of ammunition to prevent discharge
	- Raise obj against discharge
		- destroys entire Ch7 case, none of debts discharged
		- following prevent debtors from receiving discharge:
			- debtor not indiv (technically only indiv discharged, entities dissolved and debts wiped)
			- fraudulent transfers or concealment of prop
				- debtor not entitled to discharge if they concealed prop w intent to hide/delay, defraud creditor
			- unjustifiably failed to keep book/records
				- debtor not entitled to discharge if they unjustifiably concealed, falsified, failed to keep/preserve adeq bks/rec from which debtor's fin condtion or business transactions might be ascertained
			- prior discharge w/in 8yrs
				- 8yrs must elapse before another discharge can be granted
			- certain debts of *indiv* not discharged 
				- 'exceptions to discharge' see below
			- ![[Pasted image 20260114023728.png]]
	- Nondischargeable debts WAFTED under Ch7 or [[Ch 11]]
		- Willful n malicious injury
		- Alimony
		- Fraud/fines of gov
		- Taxes (w/in last 3yrs def)
		- Educational loans
		- Debts undisclosed in bankruptcy petition

Reaffirmation of Discharged Debts
- sometimes, debtor doesnt want particular debt discharged in bankruptcy
- debtor may reaffirm such debts only if agrmt to reaffirm made before granting of discharge
- like maybe if u still want good business relation

Distribution of Debtor's Estate: Pmts n Priorities
- once debtor's A's collected n liquidated, and all obj at that lvl have been disposed of, trustee dist A of estate
- 3 basic categories of claimants, paid in following order:
	1. Secured claimants
		- pmts made in full to secured claimants to extent of value of collateral securing their claims
		- claims in excess of collateral are treated like claims of other general creditors
	2. Priority claimants: SAG WEG CTI
		- money left over then used to pay first priority in full, then 2nd in full, etcc
			- if not sufficient $ to pay all creditors at a particular lvl, the creditors share pro rata
		- S - Support obligations owed to spouse/children
		- A - expenses of bankruptcy Administration, incl
			- filing/court/trustee fees
			- legal/acct fees, etc
		- G - (gap) claims accrued in ordinary course of business after involuntary petition filed (but before order of relief granted)
		- W - Wage claims of emplee for sums earned w/in 180dys of bankruptcy (up to 17,150)
		- E - sums owed for Emplee benefits (up to whatev of 17,150 left)
		- G - claims of Grain farmers/fishermen (up to 8,450)
		- C - Consumer deposits (up to 3.8k)
			- say go to store and put down deposit for item to pick up later and they go bankrupt
		- T - Tax claims (3yrs?)  - not discharged (see exceptions)
		- I - personal Injury claims arising from Intoxicated driving   - not discharged (see exceptions)
	3. General creditors who filed claims on time (pro rata)
