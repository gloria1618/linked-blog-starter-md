Reorganization: No liquidation, Trustee not req

- trustee usually not appointed
	- Exception: fraud, incompetency, etc.
- debtor remains in possession of A's n plan of reorg adopted
- creditors paid to extent possible and business continues

Voluntary n filed by debtor, but could be involuntary n filed by creditors
Available for indiv, partnerships, corps

Creation of Creditor's Committee - what's good way to reorg business?
- shortly after order for relief effective, committee of unsecured creditors appointed, usually consisting of willing persons holding *7 largest unsecured claims* against debtor
- person engaged in business other than real estate w debts not exceeding 2.6M can elect to be treated as small business
	- debtor can request creditors committee not be appointed


Equity Security Holder's Committee (for stockholders)
- if debtor is corp, equity security holders committee may be appointed w 7 largest holders of equity securities to ensure that equity security holders rec adequate rep
- commitees can:
	- consult w debtor
	- investigate debtor's finances
	- participate in prepping reorg plan, etc

Debtor Gen Remains in Possession
- committees appt to consult/advise w debtor,, but trustee gen not appt
- debtor remains in possession of debtor's As bc debtor presumed to be in best position to run business

Reorganization Plan
- debtor may file reorg plan under Ch11 any time during bankruptcy case
- unless trustee appt, debtor has exclusive right to file plan during 120dys after order for relief is effective
- other interested parties may file plan if:
	- trustee has been appt
	- debtor has not filed plan w/in 120dys after order of relief effective, or
	- debtor has filed plan but hasn't obtained acceptance of every impaired class w/in 180dys after entry of order for relief 
- Who Votes on Plan?
	- if not corp, then other creditors
	- if corp, then stockholders/creditors
	- unanimous consent not req
- Plan Contents
	- classify all claims
	- describe treatment to be accorded each impaired class
	- treat each claimant w/in particular class identically
	- est ways to implement plan
- Plan Acceptance by Creditors
	- any creditor/security holder who filed claim against debtors estate must be given oppro to accept/reject
	- only court can confirm plan, but all stockholders/creditors vote on plan
	- class of impaired claims deemed accepted if accepted by creditors holding:
		- at least 2/3 in amt
		- more than 1/2 in # of allowed claims
	- class of impaired interests deemed to have accepted plan if accepted by equity security holders (stockholders) having at least 2/3 in amt of allowed claims
- Confirmation of Plan by Court
	- court confirms plan if meets conditions like:
		- acceptance by all impaired classes (creditor/stockholders)
		- prov pmt in full for priority admin exp n gap claims
			- gap claims only for involuntary petition
		- plan feasible 
	- cram down - court can confirm plan even if not accepted by all impaired classes if:
		- at least one impaired class accepted (stockholder or creditors)
		- court finds plan not unfairly discriminatory, is fair/equitable w respect to any dissenting impaired classes
- Effects of Confirmation
	- confirmed Ch11 plan is binding on all creditors/security holders/debtor regardless of acceptance
	- gen, once confirmed the debtor pays debts according to plan
	- unless order prov otherwise, confirmation discharges debtor from all preconfirmation debts (except debts not discharged under Ch7, also not discharged under Ch11)
	- confirmation terminates automatic stay
