Who May be Debtor
- only person who resides, or has business place, in US eligible
- 'person' incl indiv, partnerships, corp, and like (IPC)
- following cannot file for bankruptcy under Ch7: RIBS
	- Railroads
	- Insurance/small business inv companies
	- Banks
	- Savings Institutions
- anyone who is debtor under Ch7 may also be debtor under Ch11, except brokers: BIBS
- Ch11 intended primarily for business debtors, but an indiv is eligible for relief 
	- Broker
	- Insurance/small business inv companies
	- Banks
	- Savings Institutions

Rights/Duties of Debt/Creditors Under 7/11
Automatic Stay
- stops almost all collection efforts
- when bankruptcy petition filed in either in/voluntary case, an automatic stay becomes effective against most creditors
- does not apply to
	- criminal prosecutions
	- paternity suits
	- cases brought to est/collect spousal/child support obligations
Duties of Debtor
- after petition filed, debtor must file, among other things:
	- list of creditors, addresses
	- schedule of AnL (at FMV or liquidation value)
	- schedule of current income/expenditures
	- stmt of debtors fin affairs
	- copies of pay stubs received w/in 60dys before filing
	- copies of fed tax returns from last tax yr. if debtor has not paid taxes for prev tax yr, they must do so before bankruptcy proceeds
- if indiv debtor in voluntary Ch7 case fails to file any of the items specified w/in 45dys after filing petition, case is auto dismissed
	- debtor must be open, honest, transparent
Voluntary Cases
- commenced by debtor filing a petition for relief
- debtor need not be insolvent to file
	- L dont need to exceed A
- [[Ch 7]] case may be dismissed if debtor has too much income
- spouses may file jointly to avoid duplicate fees
- filed voluntary petition constitutes 'an order for relief', meaning case proceeds unless court orders otherwise
Involuntary Case
- unsecured creditors may petition debtor involuntarily into bankruptcy proceedings
- creditors must show that debtor gen is not paying debts as they become due
	- 'in default': not insolvent
- ineligible debtors: farmers, charities
	- farmers, nonprofit charitable org may not be petitioned involuntarily into bankruptcy
- who must joint petition: owed at least 21,050
	- only creditors owed, indiv or in aggregate, at least 21,050 in unsecured, undisputed debt may petition debtor involuntarily
	-  # of creditors who must file depends on debtor's tot # of creditors
		- <12 creditors: 1+ owed 21,050 unsecured

Section 341 Mtg: Creditor's Mtg
- w/in 20-40dys after order for relief, mtg held
- all interested parties, incl creditors/bankruptcy trustee/debtor, must be given notice of mtg
[[Prop of Bankruptcy Estate]]
Claims Against Estate
- claims incl all rights to pmt from debtor's estate
- to have claim allowed
	- unsecured creditors must file proof of claim, n
	- shareholders must file proof of interest w bankruptcy court
- unless someone objects, filed claim/interest will auto allowed by court
- unsecured creditor failing to timely file claim may not take part in dist of debtor's estate
- gen rule: perfected security interest passes thru n survives bankruptcy even if creditor doesn't file proof of claim
Miscellaneous
- trustee can also serve as professional for estate if court approves
- trustee serving as tax preparer for estate may rec compensation as prof in addition to trustee's compensation, if court approves
