- Sales Article (Article 2) governs contracts for sale of goods (moveable things)
	- number of special rules applying to contracts for sale of goods
- Statutory law that has been widely adopted thru US

UCC Modifications Enforceable w/o Consideration
- ([[CL]], mod not enforceable even if parties agree w/o consideration)
- both parties must agree
- modification sought in good faith
- pmt by check
	- sales contract may be paid by check, unless seller demands cash, in which case seller must give reasonable time to obtain cash

Defenses
- as w [[CL]], [[Fraud]] is defense to contract under UCC: MAIDS
	- defrauded party either may sue for damages/seek recission
	- UCC statute of limitations is 4yrs
		- Common law 4-6yrs
		- action must be brought w/in 4yrs of time the cause of action accrued (from time contract breached)
- [[Statute of Frauds]]: MY LE*G*S
	- contracts for sale of goods for 500+ needs writing signed by sued party
	- Exception: SWAP
		- contracts for *Specially* manufactured goods
		- where merchant sends another merchant *Written* confirmation of contract that is sufficient to bind sender, it will also bind recipient if they dont object within 10 days
		- contracts *Admitted* in court
		- contracts *Performed*, to extent that performance has been accepted
		- ![[Pasted image 20260108160145.png]]
	- Price measured as modified
		- consideration not req to make modification enforceable so long in good faith, agreed
		- if modified, look to contract as modified to det whether writing req
			- if exceeds 500, needs writing
	- Suffice Writing
		- informal writing
			- doesnt have to be formal
			- can be stated in 1+ writing
			- letter, email, txt, etc
			- must be signed by defendant and have essential terms
		- under UCC, qty gen only essential term that cannot be omitted ([[CL]] req all essential items)
			- exceptions
				- requirements contract: seller agrees to supply all buyer's needs for period of time
				- output contract: seller agrees to sell buyer everything it produces over period of time
	- Impossibility & Impracticability
		- under contract law, if event occurs making contract obj impossible, contract discharged
		- Under Sales Article, contract will be discharged for mere impracticability
			- need only be impracticable - 'extremely more burdensome' than anticipated bc unforeseen event
			- doesnt need to be impossible
	- Failure of Agreed Upon Method of Transportation (No Defense)
		- if method of transport in contract is unavailable/commercially unreasonable, seller may use diff means of transport and buyer must accept
