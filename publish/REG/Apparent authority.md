situations where agent has power, not right to bind principal bc principal's conduct has caused 3rd parties to reasonably believe that agent had authority
- purported agent's mere rep that they r an agent is not sufficient to est apparent authority
- apparent authority req:
	- holding out by principal, or
	- negligent action by principal 
- From position
	- principal who holds another out as their agent vests agent w power to enter into all transactions that a reasonable third party would believe a person in agents position would've
	- ![[Pasted image 20260109172750.png]]
- Not affected by Secret Limiting Instructions
	- principal issuing secret instructions to agent will limit agents actual authority but not agent's apparent authority
	- must give notice to 3rd party to destroy apparent authority, must notify if agency terminated
	- apparent authority based on 3rd party reasonable beliefs, and secrets unknown to them
	- ![[Pasted image 20260109172734.png]]
- General v Special Agent
	- General agent
		- performs series of transactions involving continuity of service (eg store manager, president, CEO)
		- has broader apparent authority than special agent
	- Special agent
		- performs 1+ transactions not involving continuity of service (eg realtor)

Refresh: principal bound either way
- apparent
	- 3rd party's reasonable believe agent has power to bind principal
	- agent liable (eg breach of contract/fiduciary duty, tort)
- actual
	- agent's reasonable belief that agent has power to bind principal
	- principal cant sue agent

Termination of Apparent Authority
- when principal terminates agent's actual authority, agent cont to have apparent authority until principal notifies 3rd parties may have not known
- actual notice (oral/written) must be given to terminate apparent authority to old/current customers
- constructive notice (eg ads in newspaper/trade magazine) must be given to terminate apparent authority to potential customers who may have known of agency but had not done business w agent
- agents authority may be terminated by op of law: BID LID
	- apparent authority also terminated
	- no notice needed
