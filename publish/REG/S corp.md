[[AAA]]
- Eligibility
	- Req to qualify
		- domestic corp (not foreign entity)
		- all shareholders (non/voting) msut consent to a valid election on Form 2553, which company files w IRS
	- Shareholder limit
		- max 100 shareholders
		- fam members may elect to be treated as one shareholder
			- incl common ancestors, lineal descendants, current/former spouses of common ancestors or their lineal descendants
		- Eligible Shareholders
			- indiv, estates, certain trust types
			- qual retirement plans, 501(c)(3) charitable orgs
		- Noneligible Shareholders
			- corp, partnerships
			- nonresident aliens
	- Other testable items
		- may not be more than one class of stock outstanding
		- diff in common stock voting rights allowed
		- preferred stock not permitted
		- S corp may own interest in C corp (even 100%)
		- S corp cant file consolidated tax return w the C corp
		- S corps can own shares in C corp, but not reverse
		- Exception to S corp not being owned by another corp
			- S corp may also create a qualified S subsidiary in which S corp owns 100% of the stock
			- the 2 S corps would fiel as one entity for tax purposes 

- When S Corp Election takes Effect
	- Conditions
		- depends on if calendar/fiscal yr
		- depends if existing/newly formed corp
	- for Existing Calendar Yr Corp
		- S election filed by March15 becomes effective as of Jan1 that yr, otherwise nxt yr
		- ex
			- Form 2553 filed bw 1/1/y10 & 3/15/y10 >> effective 1/1/y10
			- Form 2553 filed after 3/15/y10 >>effective 1/1/y11
	- for Existing Non-Calendar Yr Corp
		- S election filed by 15th day of 3rd mo becomes effective on first day of fiscal yr
		- after, effective first day of nxt fiscal yr
- New Shareholders
	- after election in effect, consent of new shareholder not req
	- S corp status cont until in/voluntary termination
	- need majority of outstanding shares to vote to terminate election
	- **if any S corp shares sold to corp/partnership, S status auto terminated -- cant have em as shareholder
- S corp Tax Yr
	- file Form 1120S and must adopt calendar yr, unless valid business purpose for fiscal yr est
	- return due 15th day of 3rd mo after tax yr close
- No Tax on S corp
	- gen, no tax at corp lvl (w some exceptions)
	- S corp taxed like partnerships, no double taxation like C corp
	- can be corp lvl tax for S corp, only if it was a C corp first then converts
	- all earnings passed thru to shareholders, taxed at indiv lvl

### Termination
- terminates as result of any of following 3 occurences
	1. Shareholders holding >50% stock (non/voting) consent to voluntary revocation
	2. Fails to meet any of S status qualfications
		- corp/partnership owner
		- foreign owner
		- >100 owners (fam can count as one)
	3. Excess passive inv income: >25% of gross receipts are from passive investment inc for 3 consecutive yrs and corp has prior C corp EnP
- Effective Date
	- when majority of shareholders elect to terminate, S corp can specify effective date when revocation filed w IRS
		- no date specified, revocation filed by March 15 > effective Jan1 current yr
		- revocation after March15 >Jan1 nxt yr
	- fails to meet any req, status terminated imm
	- excess passive inc, status terminated at beginning of 4th yr
- Reelecting S Status
	- once election terminated, corp must wait until beginning of 5th yr after termination yr before can elect S status again
- Short Tax Yrs
	- when status terminated, will be S corp for part of yr n C corp for other part
	- results in 2 short tax yrs
	- short tax yr can occur if
		- corp revokes election, specifies effective date
		- corp fails to meet req for S status
	- must allocate income for yr bw S/C corp
		- allocate based on relative number of days, or
		- close books on conversion date
