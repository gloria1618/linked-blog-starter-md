Fundamental Corp Changes

Fundamental Changes
- req both board, shareholder approval thru special procedure
- unlike partnerships, don't need unanimous consent
- smthn that would change nature of corp: DAMS
	- Dissolution
		- can be pursuant to court order
		- after dissolution, corp cont to exist for purposes of winding up
		- liquidation involves
			- process of collecting corp A
			- paying exp involved
			- satisfying creditor's claims
			- dist net A of corp
	- Amendments to Articles of incorp that materially/adversely affect shareholder's rights
	- Mergers, consolidations, compulsory share exchanges
	- Sale of subs all of corp's A outside regular course of business
- Gen Procedure
	1. Board Resolution
		- initiates fundamental change to corp
		- majority of BoD must adopt resolution setting forth proposed action, submitting it for vote at shareholder's mtg
	2. Notice
		- corp must notify all shareholders even if not entitled to vote
	3. Shareholder Approval
		- chng must be approved by majority of shares voted at mtg
	4. Filing of Articles
		- doc setting forth action taken (referred to as 'article') must be:
			- executed by corp
			- filed w state
	5. Right to Dissent/Appraisal Rights
		- shareholders who have right to vote on fundamental corp change typically have a right to dissent/appraisal right if:  (sell bc thinks value going down)
			- shareholder votes against fundamental change
			- it is nevertheless approved

- Amendments to Articles of Incorporation
	- corp may amend articles of incorp in any/as many respects as desired, as long as provisions as amended are lawful
	- corp may amend articles to give/stop preemptive rights/change broader purpose of clause
- Merger
	- A&B > A
	- involves 1+ corp joining w another
	- on corp survives merger and cont existence, while other merging corps cease to exist
	- surviving corp has all rights, L, obligations of merged corps
	- ![[Pasted image 20260115232633.png]]
- Consolidation
	- A&B > C
	- involves 1+ corp joining together to form new corp
	- each constituent corp ceases to exist after consolidation
	- new corp L for debts of old corp
- Share Exchange
	- A+B =A+B
	- A owns all shares of B, both exist as separate companies 
	- transaction where one corp acquires all outstanding shares of 1+ classes of stock of another corp
	- both corp cont to exist as separate entities
	- when share exchange takes place, shares are exchanged as plan provides, and holders entitled only to rights of the exchanged shares
- Gen Procedure (except short form merger)
	- both corps in merger and all corps involv in consolidation must follow gen procedure for fundamental corp changes set out prev
	- notice must incl summary of plan of merger, consolidation, share exchange, etc.
	- special rules for share exchange
		- only corp whose shares being acquired need follow fundamental change procedure
	- plan of merger/share exchange must incl
		- terms/conditions of plan
		- manner of converting corp's securities
- Gen Procedure: Merger of Subsidiary (short form merger)
	- parent corp owning 90%+ of subsidiary corp may merge subsidiary into parent w/o approval of shareholders of either corp or subsidiary's board
	- however, parent must mail copy of plan to each shareholder who has not waived this right
- Gen Procedure: Fending off Unwanted Takeover Attempts
	- if corp faced w prospect of being taken over and BoD wants to resist attempt, it may do so in many ways, incl:
		- persuading shareholders to reject offer
		- suing person/company attempting takeover for misrep/omission and obtain an injunction against takeover
		- merging w white knight (company w which directors want to merge)
		- making a 'self tender' 
			- offer to acquire stock from its own stockholders, thus retain control in order to prevent takeover by voting against?
		- paying 'greenmail'
			- ie pay person/company to abandon takeover attempt
		- locking up crown jewels
			- ie give 3rd party option to purchase company's most valuable A's
		- undertaking 'scorched earth' policy
			- sell off A's or take out loans that would make company less financially attractive
		- applying 'shark repellent'
			- amending articles of incorp/bylaws to make takeover more difficult

Calendar Yr vs Fiscal Yr
- companies typically have option of choosing
	- calendar yr as yr end
	- a fiscal yr end 
- for tax purposes, fiscal yr must first be approved by IRS

Foreign Corp Must Qualify
- foreign corp
	- is corp created under laws of another state
	- must obtain certificate of authority from each state in which it does intrastate business
- maintaining an office in foreign state would be ex of doing business in foreign state
- merely maintaining bank acct, collecting debt, hiring emplee in foreign state are not instances of doing business in foreign state sufficient to trigger the qualification req
