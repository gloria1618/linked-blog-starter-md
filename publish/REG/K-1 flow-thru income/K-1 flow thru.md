Separately Stated Items
- Certain items of inc, gain/loss, deduction that receive special treatment at indiv lvl are carved out + flowed thru separately to owner
- common separately stated items incl:
	- rental real estate inc/loss
	- interest inc
	- dividend inc
	- royalties
	- net ST CG/L
	- net LT CG/L
	- net section 1231 gain/loss
	- charitable contributions
	- sec 179 exp deduction
	- guaranteed pmts to partners (partnerships only)
		- > compensation paid by partnership to partner, either for services rendered/ use of capital, det w/o regard to partnership inc (which is also split)
			- pmts are business exp reducing partnership ordinary inc/loss
			- flow thru as separately stated item of income on recipient partner's Schedule K-1 & incl in partner's taxable gross inc
		- distributions to partners/shareholders in S corp
			- not taxable inc
			- partnership/S corp income taxed to owners when earned, regardless of whether/when dist to owners

###### Self empl Inc
Guaranteed pmts for services
- to partners are self empl inc to recipient
- subj to self empl (SS n Medicare) tax in addition to inc tax
- shareholder in S corp receives salary, rather than guaranteed pmt
- as shareholder is employed by s corp and not self empl
	- .5 of SS n Medicare taxes paid by corp
	- .5 withheld from shareholders salary
Owner's Share of Ord Business Inc
- Partnership
	- partner's allocable share of partnership ord bus inc is self empl inc subj to self empl tax if partner is actively involved in op 
	- if not actively involved (eg limited partner not allowed to participate in managing), then partner's allocable share of ord bus inc is not self empl inc
- S corp
	- share holders allocable share of S corp ordinary business inc is not self empl inc, regardless of involvement... its wages
		- if dont want wages n j want share of income, still no self empl tax
	- avoidance of self empl tax is reason that business may choose to org as S corp rather than partnership

###### Tax Basis Loss Limitation

- inc
