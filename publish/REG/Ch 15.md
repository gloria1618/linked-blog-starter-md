Ancillary & Cross Border Cases

- US adoption of Model Law on Cross Border Insolvency promulgated by the UN
- adopted to promote uniform/coordinated legal regime for cross border insolvency cases
- go after A in other countries

Commencement of Ancillary Proceeding
- Ch15 ancillary case commenced by 'foreign rep' filing a petition for recognition of a 'foreign proceeding'
- operates as principal door of a foreign rep to US courts
- petition must show:
	- existence of foreign proceeding
	- appt n authority of foreign rep
- after notice of hearing, US court authorized to issue order recog foreign proceedign as either:
	- 'foreign main proceeding'
	- 'foreign non-main proceeding'

Foreign Rep's Powers
- authorized to op debtor's business
- once recognized, can
	- seek additional relief from bankruptcy court
	- authorized to bring full blown (as opposed to ancillary) bankruptcy case under Ch7/11
- Foreign rep may
	- participate in pending US insolvency case
	- intervene in any other US case in which debtor is party

Prohibition against Discrimination
- prohibits discrimination against foreign creditors (except certain foreign gov/tax claims, which may be governed by treaty)
- Req of Notice/Cooperation
	- req notice to foreign creditors concerning a US bankruptcy case, incl notice of right to file claims
	- US courts n trustees must 'coop to max extent possible' w foreign courts/reps
