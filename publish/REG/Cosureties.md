- 2+ sureties of same obligation n jointly/severally L in event debtor defaults
	- ie any one or more may be L for entire obligation
	- creditor can go after all sureties as group, jointly, indiv. each surety L to creditor for whatev amt they promise to guarantee

Exoneration vs Contribution
- Exoneration - before surety pays creditor
	- action brought in by surety against cosureties before sureties pay creditor
	- suit to compel pmt (not against principal debtor) but against other solvent cosureties
	- compels cosureties to pay their pro rata shares of debt
	- involves mult 3rd parties guaranteeing that debt
- Contribution - after surety pays creditor
	- action brought in by surety against cosureties after surety pays creditor
	- compels cosureties to pay pro rata shares of debt
	- involves suing other solvent cosureties for contribution only after surety has paid creditor their promised amt

Solvent v Insolvent
- if contract doesn't specify L of each surety, each surety L for pro rata share det by # of solvent sureties

- L of Solvent Surety
	1. Each solvent surety L to creditor for 100% of unpaid debt
		- if no $ amt specified, each surety L for 100% of unpaid debt
		- if jointly/severally L, creditor may pursue each surety indiv or hold all of them jointly/severally L for unpaid debt
	2. Each solvent cosurety is L to surety that paid creditor for their pro rata share of debt
		-  if no $ amt specified, each surety is L for pro rata share det by # of solvent sureties 

Cosureties Obligation for Varying Amts
- where cosureties obligated for varying amts by agrmts & debt is reduced by part pmt by principal, each cosurety remains L for orgininal amt stated in agrmt 
	- but pmt >pro rata share of reduced debt entitles cosurety to contribution from solvent cosureties for excess in proportion of amts of original L
	- contribution is done after creditor is paid the exoneration
