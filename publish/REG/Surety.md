Suretyship transaction involves 3 parties:
- creditor (lender)
- principal debtor (borrower)
- surety
	- one who agrees to be L for debt/obligation of another

Surety v Guarantor 
- Surety
	- directly L on contract
- Guarantor
	- L to creditor only if debtor doesn't perform duty to creditor
	- guarantor of collectability L only if creditor unable to collect from debtor after exhausting all legal remedies, incl
		- demand
		- suit
		- judgement
		- exhaustion of all supplementary proceedings

Creditor's Rights outside Suretyship
- debtor has few options to alleviated debt if doesnt have funds to pay creditor
	- debtor files for bankruptcy - voluntary petition
	- creditor can push debtor into bankruptcy - involuntary petition
- besides filing bankruptcy petition, debtor can
	- enter into a creditor's composition, or
		- agrmt bw debtor n 2+ creditors that debtor pays the creditors less than their full claims in full satisfaction of their claims 
		- contract consideration arises from agrmt by each creditor w each other to take less than their full claim
			- results in debtor being discharged in full for debts owed the participating creditors after debtor has paid agreed amt
	- make assignment for benefit of creditors
		- debtor transfers some or all of property to trustee who
			- disposes of property, and
			- uses proceeds to satisfy debtor's debts
		- debtor not discharged from unpaid debts since creditors do not agree to any discharge
