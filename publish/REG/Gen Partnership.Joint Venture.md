Formation of General Partnership
- when 2+ ppl intend to carry on as co-owners for business profit
- papers not req, nothing needs to be filed
	- if partners want to enforce agrmt to remain partners for 1yr+, writing req under Statute of Frauds
- express agrmt not req
	- agrmt can be implied from conduct
	- adv - ease of formation

vs Joint Venture
- courts sometimes try to distinguish JV from GP, but legal req/consequences/adv/disadv are identical
- GP is ongoing business
- for exam, key diff is that JV formed for
	- single transaction/project (hotdog stand for parage)
	- related series of transactions/projects
- if unclear if intended to enter into partnership, an agrmt to share profits gives rise to presumption that partnership intended
	- if u lend $ as creditor, u would collect principal/interest but not profits
	- if u rec share of profits, u could be considered partner

Adv of GP v Corp
- Partnerships
	- treated as entities for most purposes (can own land, sue, be sued, etc)
	- are not taxable entities for inc tax purposes

Op of GP
- absent agrmt to contrary, all partners have equal rights to manage 
- mgmt rights n voting power are not based on amt contributed
	- gen rule: majority vote based on # of partners
	- ![[Pasted image 20260115025938.png]]
- Req Approval
	- decisions regarding matters w/in ord course of partnership's business may be controlled by majority vote unless partnership agrmt prov otherwise
	- no partner by virtue of title as partner has apparent authority to act indiv
	- matters outside ord course of business req consent of all partners (unanimous)
		- ex: admitting new partners, 
		- confessing a judgement (admitting L in lawsuit) or submitting claim to arbitration
		- making fundamental chng in business (eg sale of business, purchase another business, expansion into new products)
		- ![[Pasted image 20260115030323.png]]
- Agency Law Governs
	- every partner is agent of partnership for purpose of business, and partnership is principal
	- act of partner apparently carrying on in ord course of business the business of partnership will bind partnership thru apparent authority
	- if partner acts w/o actual/apparent authority, partnership can still become bound if it knows material facts of transaction n ratifies, either
		- expressly
		- by accepting benefits of transaction
	- partner who acts outside scope of actual authority is in breach of contract and partnership can hold that partner L for breach of fiduciary duty

Rights of Partners
- in Partnership Prop
	- partnership owns all $/prop contributed to partnership by partners and all other prop acq by partnership
	- partners do not own partnership prop
		- as gen rule, partners have no right to possess/use partnership prop other than for partnership purposes
			- an indiv partner may not assign/sell partnership prop for own benefit
			- partner's personal creditors cannot attach partnership prop to satisfy indiv partner's debt
			- ![[Pasted image 20260115031820.png]]
- in Partnership Interest
	- partner may assign their interest in profits/surplus at any time
	- assignee obtains right to rec partner's share of profits but doesnt become partner, so no right to
		- attend partnership mtgs
		- inspect partnership books/records
		- vote
	- assignee can obtain such rights only if admitted to the partnership as partner, which gen req unanimous partner approval
	- charging order: creditors may attach a partner's interest 
		- creditor of indiv partner may obtain from court a charging order against an indiv partners share of profits
	- upon death, heirs entitled to deceased partner's share of profits
		- partner's right to partnership prop vests in surviving partners
	- every partner has right to inspect/copy bks/records of partnership
	- ![[Pasted image 20260115032125.png]]

Duties/Legal Obligations of Partners
- Fiduciary Duties Owed to Other Partners
	- LORA: loyalty, obedience, reasonable care, account
- Each Partner Personally L for All Partnership Obligations
	- all contracts entered into
	- all torts committed by other partners w/in scope of partnership business or which are otherwise authorized 
	- partner's L is joint/several
		- each partner personally n individually L for entire amt of all partnership obligations
	- in many states, however, a creditor cannot satisfy a judgement against an individual partner unless:
		- partner named in lawsuit
		- A's of partnership are exhausted

Termination
- Dissociation of GP
	- dissociation - chng in relationship of partners caused by any partner ceasing to be assoc in carrying on business
	- dissociation doesnt necessarily cause a dissolution n winding up of business
	- partnership at will may be rightfully dissolved by:
		- partners notice of withdrawal
		- dissociation at any time
			- if partner wrongly dissociates, L for breach
			- say like written agrmt for 3yrs
	- any remaining partners could agree to continue business, but as new partnership entity
	- Events of Dissociation: partner dissociates from partnership when they ...
		- give notice of withdrawal
		- die
		- becomes bankrupt
		- expelled
		- event occurs that was set out in agrmt as event that would cause dissociation
	- Consequences
		- right to participate in mgmt ceases and actual authority terminates
		- dissociated partner's apparent authority to bind partnership cont until 3rd parties notified
	- Dissociated Partner's L to Other Parties
		- gen, dissociated partner remains L for debts incurred by partnership prior to dissociation unless there has been:
			- release by creditor
			- novation
		- dissociated partner may be held L for partnership debts incurred for up to 2yrs after dissociation unless partner gives notice of dissociation
		- if new partner admitted, new partner not personally L for debts incurred by partnership before becoming partner
- Dissolution of GP
	- Events Causing Dissolution
		- gen, partnership dissolved and business must be wound up if:
			- partnership is at will and partner gives notice of withdrawal
				- only one partner must quit to dissolve a partnership
			- partners agree to dissolution
			- court orders dissolution
		- death of partner doesn't cause dissolution if remaining partners agree to cont w/in 90dys of death
		- ![[Pasted image 20260115044452.png]]
	- Partnership Cont after Dissolution
		- partnership cont to exist after dissolution until business wound up, at which time partnership is terminated
			- ex: each partner will cont to have apparent authority to bind partnership, and each partner will cont to be L for obligations of partnership
		- partnership terminated only after winding up process complete

Distribution of A - Final Acct
- Application
	- Amts Due/Owed
		- to det amts due/owed, deduct from A left upon dissolution:
			- any amts owed to creditors (incl partners who r creditors)
			- amts needed to return partner's contributions (if not alr repaid)
	- Divide Profit (if any)
		- if A at dissolution are less than needed to pay creditors n return contributions, then there's loss that must be divided among partners
		- either case, unless partnership agrmt prov otherwise
			- profits div equally
			- loss div same as profits
- Order of Dist
	- when solvent partnership dissolved n A reduced to cash, cash must be used to pay partnership's L in following order:
		- Creditors
			- incl partners who r creditors, must be paid before non creditors
		- Partners
			- after creditor obligations satisfied, each partner entitled to pmt, first to return contributions, then on acct of profits
