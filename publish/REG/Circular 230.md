Treasury Dep Circular230 in IRS publication entitled
'Regulations Governing Practice before the IRS'
- "practice before the IRS"
	- comms w IRS for txpyr
	- repping a txpyr
	- prep/filing needed docs w IRS for txpyr

- Circular 230 addresses: 'ADS Rules' for discipline
	- rules gov Authority to prac before the IRS
	- Duties/restrictions relating to prac before the IRS
	- Sanctions for violation of regulations
	- Rules applicable to disciplinary hearings
- 
- Subpart A: Authority to Practice
	- rules governing practice before IRS apply to 
		- unlimited representation rights before the IRS (need not have prepped/signed ur return)
			- attorney's
			- CPA
			  enrolled agents
		- enrolled actuaries
		- enrolled retirement plan agents
		- registered tax return preparers 
		- other ppl allowed to rep txpyr before IRS in limited circumstances
			- eg officer/FT emplee of corp/trust may rep that corp/trust
		- **anyone who prep/assists in prepping federal tax return for compensation must register w IRS
- Subpart B: Duties/Restrictions in Practice before the IRS
	- 10.20 info to be furnished to IRS
		- practitioner may withhold info/rec they believe in good faith to be privileged
		- must inform IRS if know of anyone that possesses IRS requested info
	- 10.21 knowledge of client omission
		- practitioner knowing of client's noncompliance w fed tax laws/error/omission on tax return/other doc must
			- notify client orally/in writing, advise client of penalties
			- advise client of consequences under tax law of noncompliance/error/omission 
		- Common wrong answers
			- dont need to withdraw from client until they correct error
			- no need to notify IRS about client omission/error
	- 10.22 diligence as to accuracy
		- practitioner must exercise due diligence in 
			- prep, approving, filing tax returns, other docs related to IRS matters
			- det correctness of oral/written rep made by practitioner to clients/Treasury dep
		- practitioner presumed to have exercised due diligence = rely on work product of another person if practitioner used reasonable care w respect to reliance
			- reasonable reliance on work product of others is ok 
	- 10.23 prompt disposition of pending matters
		- no practitioner may unreasonably delay any matter before the IRS
	- 10.24 assistance from/to disbarred/suspended persons + former IRS emplees
		- practitioner cannot accept help from or assist ppl who r under disbarment/suspended from practice before the IRS
	- 10.25 practice by former gov emplees, their partners/associates 
		- no member of firm where former gov emplee works can rep txpyr where maybe conflict of interest, unless firm isolates former gov emplee from representation
			- if gov emplee personally/substantially participated in a particular manner, that indiv can never represent/assist those parties
				- most restrictive
			- if gov emplee had 'official responsibility' for particular matter, they cannot rep those parties for 2yrs
			- if gov emplee participated in rule dev, they cannot appear before IRS regarding rule for 1yr
				- least restrictive
	- 10.26 Notary
		- practitioner may not act as notary public for their clients on any tax matter
	- 10.27 Fees
		- practitioner cannot charge unconscionable fee in connection w matters before the IRS 
		- unconscionable
			- grossly unfair relative to industry standard
		- contingent fee allowed in following 3 situations before the IRS:
			- audit: IRS examination of an original tax return
			- claim for refund of interest/penalties
			- judicial proceeding 
	- 10.28 Return of Client's Records 
		- practitioner owes client a duty of confidentiality
			- E: peer review
		- practitioner MUST promptly return client rec needed for client to comply w fed tax obligations, even if there's fee dispute
			- may keep copies of rec
		- client rec incl
			- materials prepped by client/3rd party prov to practitioner
			- any return, refund claim, doc prepped by practitioner 
		- Fee Dispute
			- if state law allows practitioner to retain client rec in case of fee dispute 
				- practitioner must still return any rec that must be attached to returns
				- any additional rec may be retained but practitioner must give client reasonable access to review/copy, if necessary for client to comply w fed tax oblig
	- 10.29 Conflict of Interest
		- bw clients, spouses, partners
		- practitioner may not rep client if involv conflict of interest unless:
			- practitioner believes they can competently rep each of clients
			- no state/fed law prohibits rep
			- each client waives conflict of interest, gives informed consent, confirms waiver in writing w.in 30 dys after giving informed consent 
	- 10.30 Solicitation 
		- No false/misleading info
			- Ad/Solicitation Restrictions - no guarantee u will eliminate/reduce taxes
				- no false/fraudulent/misleading/deceptive when advertising
				- if claiming expertise, credentials must be prov
		- Written Schedule of Fees - practitioner must honor fees for 30dys following last date fees were publicized
		- Comms Fee Info
			- can be comms in many ways
				- radio, tv, mail, ecommerce comms
			- copies of comms must be retained for 36mo
	- 10.31 Negotiation of Txpyr Checks
		- practitioner may not endorse/negotiate any refund check issued to client
		- holding for safekeeping ok 
	- 10.33 Best Practices for Tax Advisors
		- prov client w highest quality rep
		- comms engagement terms to det client's purpose/use for advice
		- facts/conclusions must be supported by law
			- due diligence
		- advise client abt importance of conclusions reached
			- ex: whether client can avoid penalties
		- act fairly, w integrity in prac before the IRS
		- reasonable steps to ensure all firm members, assoc follow these procedures
	- 10.35 Competence
		- must possess necessary competence to practice before the IRS
			- competent practice req approp lvl of knowledge, skill, thoroughness, prep
		- may become competent thru various means
			- consulting w experts
			- studying relevant law
