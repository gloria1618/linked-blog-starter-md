- both models historically used to mng software dev, other technical IT proj, but principles flexible enough to be applied more gen to modern chng mgmt practices across diff org functions
- can be implemented in disciplines such as finance/logisitics to optimize managing chng
- primary diff bw models is flexibility


- Waterfall
	- diff teams of emplees performing sep tasks in sequence, w each team
		- beginning work from pre-written authoritative agrmt of preceding team, and
		- ending work when business reqs for team have been met
	- proj then passes to next team
		- teams work linearly 
	- challenges
		- req lots of time to complete
		- benefits of new sys not realized until completion
		- no customer input, chng can be difficult to mng
		- some emplees may be idle before beginning/after completing their step
	1. Plan
	2. Analyze
	3. Design
	4. Develop
	5. Test 
	6. Deploy
	7. Maintain
- Agile
	- created to address waterfall issues
	- more flexible approach to chng mgmt
	- cross functional teams, each dedicated to particular func/imprvments of sys drawn from prioritized list of customer's remaining needs for sys
	- diff teams work on diff phases/tasks simultaneously
		- shorter deadlines for efficiency, comms critical
	- allows chngs of direction thruout proj, acct for stakeholder feedback thru consistent comms, and any chngs in tech landscape that can occur over course of an extensive proj
	- final outcome proj can evolve thruout the proj's life cycle 
	- Principles
		- satisfy customer w early/cont delivery of highest priority features
		- welcome chng- chng request is oppro to be closer to customer needs
		- deliver working software freq, working software is primary measure of progress
		- complete only work requested by customer
		- conduct short, freq, regular, mtgs to maintain focus/make adj
